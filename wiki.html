Willow Te Aroha Green (Born April 19, 2000) is a New Zealand-American politician, who is new to Capitol Hill. Previously attending Harvard University, graduating summa cum laude with a degree in Comparative Literature and Sociology, she is described as a "successful academic" and "a force to be reckoned with".

Born to Kiwi father Cai Green and American mother Amelie Yates, Willow became fluent in both English and Maori from a young age. She lived in New Zealand for 14 years, before her family migrated to the United States, to allow Willow's mother to be close to her own mother, who had tragically fallen ill.

Willow is a avid reader, having read all of Shakespeare's plays and memorised Hamlet's soliloquy. She is a fan of Thriller and Crime fiction and has said her favourite book is The Girl on the Train. Willow is considered fluent in English, Maori, Spanish and German, and enjoys reading literature and listening to music in multiple languages.

In the past, Green has struggled with Anxiety, and nearly missed out on her academic success due to a prolonged absence from school. Her closest friend Cai mentored her through and helped her return to school and finish her studies which allowed her to graduate High School and attend Harvard.

Green describes herself as a Democratic Socialist, she places her values on welfare, the environment and democracy.
